NFC Payments Explained

The technology powering tap-to-pay cards, phones and wearables.

Compare Contactless-Ready Cards

What is NFC?

NFC (Near Field Communication) enables ultra-short-range wireless transfer between your card or wallet and a payment terminal – typically less than 4 cm.

  • Built into contactless cards
  • Built into phones (Apple Pay, Google Pay, Samsung Pay)
  • Built into watches, rings and bands
  • Uses encrypted, tokenized card data

How NFC payment flow works

1. Card / device → terminal

Your device shares a one-time cryptogram instead of your full card number (PAN).

2. Terminal → acquirer

The terminal sends encrypted transaction data to the payment processor.

3. Network & issuer

Visa/Mastercard and your bank run risk checks and decide approve or decline in milliseconds.

4. Approval

The POS confirms the transaction – and you're done.

NFC security layers

  • Single-use cryptograms (dynamic security codes)
  • No raw card number shared directly with the merchant
  • Wallets require biometrics or device PIN
  • Wearables use tokenized card profiles
  • Short physical range reduces skimming risk

Why some cards tap better than others

Not all tap experiences feel the same:

  • NFC antenna size and placement vary between issuers
  • Card material and thickness affect range
  • Different terminals behave differently in the field
  • Some issuers prioritise mobile wallets over plastic performance

Choose the best tap-ready cards

If you depend on tap (especially when travelling), look for cards with:

  • Strong wallet support (Apple Pay, Google Pay, Samsung Pay)
  • No foreign-transaction fees
  • High reliability on global terminals
  • Rewards on contactless and mobile wallet transactions
